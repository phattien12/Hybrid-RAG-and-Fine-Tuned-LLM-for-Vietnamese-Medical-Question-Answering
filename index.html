Abstract

Abstract— Multimodal computer vision has emerged as a critical research direction aiming to bridge the gap between visual understanding and natural language processing. Traditional computer vision systems are primarily designed to perform isolated tasks such as object detection, classification, or segmentation, often lacking the ability to reason about visual content in a human-like manner. In contrast, multimodal approaches integrate visual and textual modalities, enabling systems to both interpret images and generate meaningful linguistic outputs.
